Main attraction in my hometown

I am Terengganu's resident. I was born, raised and originated there. I would love to present the most famous main attraction of my hometown to you.





The Terengganu State Museum


The Terengganu State Museum is the largest museum in Malaysia about 75,000 square metres of expansive floor space is divided over 4 connected towers with unique traditional terengganu craftmanship which houses 9 type of galleries. The 9 galleries are Natural History Gallery, Textile Gallery, Royal Gallery, Craft Gallery, Petroleum Gallery, Islamic Gallery, Historical Gallery, Seafaring & Trade Gallery and Fisheries & Marine Gallery.









The Crystal Mosque


The Crystal Mosque also known as Masjid Kristal by the locals. The mosque can accommodate over 1,500 worshipper. Masjid Kristal not only a unique mosque but one of the tourist attraction in Terengganu for they house world famous mosque replicas known as Islamic Heritage Park. Besides Masjid Kristal, there are many other beautiful mosque in Terengganu. For example, there are Floating Mosque, Masjid Ulul Albab, the Eight Dome Mosque, Surau Langgar Rindu and many others.









Hawskbill Turtle

Most tourist came to see the turtle in Terengganu. There are two enormous turtle stones (Batu Penyu), one of which is immersed in seawater and the other is hidden between bushes on a small hill in a settlement away from the coast, are thought to be the attraction for turtles, according to the locals. The legendary "Batu Penyu" turtle, which is said to be found in Bukit Che Hawa, Rantau Abang, is thought to be the turtle queen that lured the enormous leatherback dermochelys coriacea turtles to the beach at Rantau Abang every year from July to August to lay eggs.

Despite the Fisheries Department's conservation efforts, studies indicate a decline in the number of turtles returning each year to lay their eggs. A visitor's incorporative behaviour, such as yanking the turtle's flippers or riding on its back, has contributed to this in part.The nearly extinct leatherback turtles travelled as far as South America to lay their eggs. However, the whole population of turtles, including the Green Back and Hawksbill species that swim to our coasts, is impacted by brisk development and poaching, and attempts are being done to stem their declining population. The Rantau Abang Turtle Information Centre offers information on the many turtle species, and the turtle hatchlings can be seen on the beach across the street. And if you're fortunate, you'll get to see as young turtles are released into the water.









Kenyir Dam

Kenyir Dam Historical event. There used to be a village at the bottom of Lake Kenyir, was once home to nine villages, including Kampung Melur, Kelitok, Berching, Petang, Mentong, Melaka, Kuala Terenggan, Kerbak, and Belimbing. The village was shifted into three main settlements, Kampung Melur, Kelitok, and Berching, with a total population of around 100 families. The village was connected by river and boat or raft as the main means of transportation, with an average of four people per family. The village's remote location and isolation from development made it difficult for residents to commute to Kuala Berang. In the 1950s, the state government decided to develop Lake Kenyir as a water catchment area and tourist attraction. Residents were asked to leave, although they received fair compensation to ensure their welfare.









Kenyir Lake

Kenyir Lake is the largest artificial lake in Malaysia which was developed in 1985 as a result of Kenyir River damming. It serves as an alternate entrance to Taman Negara or the National Park and is the largest man-made lake in Southeast Asia. The lake can typically hold 13.6 billion cubic metres of water. While the deepest point is 145 metres below the surface. Tasik Kenyir is currently undergoing rapid development and offers a variety of tourist attractions, including the Bird Park, Herb Park, Tropical Park, and Duty Free Island on Bayas.









Redang Island

Swimming, snorkelling, and scuba diving are all very popular on the island. Terumbu Kili, which has a rocky seascape covered in both soft and hard corals at around 15 metres and slopes down to 20 metres at the sandy bottom, is one of the well-known locations. Another destination not to be missed is Tanjung Tokong. Besides, there are other island to not be missed for its beauty and attraction such as Kapas Island, Perhentian Island, Lang Tengah Island, Gemia Island, Tenggol Island and Bidong Island.









Kuala Terengganu Drawbridge

The Kuala Terengganu Drawbridge spans the mouth of the Terengganu River in Kuala Terengganu, Terengganu, Malaysia. The bridge, which spans the river as the fourth in the Kuala Terengganu City region after Sultan Mahmud Bridge, Manir Bridge, and Pulau Sekati Bridge, was officially opened on August 1, 2019, and its design was influenced by London's Tower Bridge. The drawbridge crosses the mouth of the Terengganu River over a 638-meter-long, 23-meter-wide single-carriageway.It links Muara Utara (North Bank) in Seberang Takir with Muara Selatan (South Bank), a reclaimed land in the heart of Kuala Terengganu's city, close to Tanjung. When the double-leafed span is closed, it has a 12.5 metre clearance above the water. The drawbridge's clear span is 50 metres long. To view Terengganu people can get inside the bridge tower with entry ticket as low as Rm5 the last time I remember.









Batu Bersurat

The oldest carvings and the first examples of Jawi writing in Malaysia may be discovered on the inscribed stone known as Batu Bersurat Terengganu, which is thought to be 700 years old. It is proof that Islam was practised in Terengganu prior to 1326 or 1386. The stone was found in Kampung Buluh, Kuala Berang, Hulu Terengganu, in 1887 following a flood on the banks of Sungai Tersat. Arab businessman Sayid Husin bin Ghulam al-Bokhari made the discovery after coming across the stone by chance following a flood. The stone attests to Terengganu's governmental recognition of Islam as a well-established religion. The Terengganu Stone Inscription is a documented legal code that attests to the existence of the Islamic legal order and serves as a repository for Islamic history in Malay historiography. The Jawi writing system, language, astronomy, and epigraphy are four aspects of the engraved stones that show they were influenced by Islam. The original Batu Bersurat are currently situated at Kuala Berang, Terengganu. The real Batu Bersurat are currently at Kuala Berang, Hulu Terengganu.









Bukit Keluang

Some of Malaysia's best coastline beauty may be found in Bukit Keluang, which features magnificent beaches, a wooden walkway along the cliff edge, sea caves, and a 141-meter-high hill with a stunning perspective of the South China Sea. It became the tourist attraction spot contributed by the locals as it is their exercise spot. Besides exercise, they get to take beautiful picture from up there with beautiful sea view as their background. The government saw the potential of that place thus there are improvement made at the surrounding area.









Lata Payung

Lata Payung are known as one of beautiful picnic and bath location with its clear blue water lagoon. However it is advisable to not go there in monsoon season and better check the weather on normal days to avoid being ovurrun by the headwater or known as kepala air by the locals. There are more picnic location with waterfall, Chemerong Waterfall, Sekayu Waterfall, Langsir Waterfall, Tok Dor Waterfall, Puteri Waterfall, Tanggol Waterfall, Jenagor Waterfall, Lasir Waterfall, Saok Waterfall, Lata Kolam, Lata Belatan, Lata Tembakah and many others. My favourite spot however are the La Hotspring in Besut.
